# SciLab — IB Grade 6 Science App 🔬

A mobile-friendly science learning app for IB MYP Year 1 (Grade 6) students. Built as a single HTML file that works like a native app on iPhone and iPad.

---

## What it does

SciLab helps Grade 6 IB students learn science during holidays with five built-in screens:

- **Home** — Unit overview cards for all 6 IB science topics with progress tracking
- **Daily** — 6 hands-on activities to do at home, with XP rewards for completing them
- **Quiz** — 5 science questions with instant feedback and explanations
- **Lab** — 5 home experiments using everyday kitchen materials
- **Ask Claude** — AI-powered science tutor that answers any Grade 6 science question

---

## How to use it

### On iPhone or iPad (recommended)

1. Open the link in **Safari**
2. Tap the **Share** button (box with arrow)
3. Tap **Add to Home Screen**
4. Tap **Add**

The app now appears on your home screen with an icon, just like a real app — no App Store needed.

### On Android

1. Open the link in **Chrome**
2. Tap the three-dot menu
3. Tap **Add to Home Screen**

### On a computer

Just open the link in any browser — Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Edge.

## Home experiments included

1. **Density tower** — layer 5 liquids to see density in action
2. **Lung capacity test** — measure lung volume with a balloon
3. **Chromatography art** — separate ink pigments with water
4. **Pendulum physics** — investigate what affects a pendulum's period
5. **Seed germination** — test variables that affect plant growth

---

## XP and streak system

- Complete a daily activity → earn **10 XP**
- Answer a quiz question correctly → earn **5 XP**
- Your streak and XP are shown at the top of the app

---

## Powered by Claude AI

The **Ask** screen connects to Anthropic's Claude AI to give friendly, age-appropriate answers to any science question. The AI is set up as an IB Grade 6 tutor — it gives clear explanations, uses simple language, and connects concepts to real life.

## For parents

This app is a supplement to school learning, not a replacement. It works best when children:

- Do one daily activity each day of the holidays
- Write up experiments like proper IB lab reports
- Use the Ask screen when they are stuck on a concept from class

No personal data is collected. The only external connection is to the Anthropic API when a question is asked in the Ask screen.
